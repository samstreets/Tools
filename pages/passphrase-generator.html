Why Passphrases?

Easy to Remember

A passphrase like "Correct-Horse-Battery-Staple" is far easier to memorise than "xK#9mP!2". The human brain naturally processes and retains word sequences.

Genuinely Secure

Four random words from a large wordlist gives ~51 bits of entropy. Six words gives ~78 bits — more than most complex password policies require.

Easy to Type

Passphrases avoid cryptic special characters. They're faster to type on mobile, less prone to errors, and work well with most authentication systems.

NIST Guidelines

NIST (SP 800-63B) recommends passphrases of at least 15 characters and advises against complexity rules and mandatory rotation — length and memorability are key.
